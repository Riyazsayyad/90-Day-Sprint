Why is the Treeify Threshold 8?

The JDK source code comments explain that under random hash codes, the sizes of buckets follow a Poisson distribution. With a default load factor of 0.75, the probability of a bucket having a size of 8 is extremely small (about 1 in 10 million):

List size occurrences probability:
0:    0.60653066
1:    0.30326533
2:    0.07581633
3:    0.01263606
4:    0.00157952
5:    0.00015795
6:    0.00001316
7:    0.00000094
8:    0.00000006
more: less than 1 in ten million

Therefore, under normal usage, treeification should rarely happen. It acts as a safety net against poorly written hash functions and HashDoS security attacks.
